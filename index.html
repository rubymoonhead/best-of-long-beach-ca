Long Beach, California

This coastal charter city on the southern part of Los Angeles County, and formerly known as Wilmore City whose growing population voted, in 1897, to incorporate and rename it City of Long Beach sits on top of Long Beach Oil Field. Waterfront attractions are the RMS Queen Mary (retired 1967), a British ocean liner built in Scotland, and The Aquarium of the Pacific, a public aquarium that spotlights organisms and seascapes of the Pacific.

Things to do

Purchase general admission tickets to the Aquarium of the Pacific. Enjoy the view of Long Beach from the sky in a private helicopter tour of Rancho Palos Verdes, Los Angeles, and Long Beach. Round-trip travel to Catalina Island with Catalina Express ferry service. Rent a Swan Boat at Rainbow Lagoon. Go on a full day tour sightseeing major sights without driving from Long Beach to Los Angeles. Ride on your own bike and join a regular meet up for free for a Long Beach Fitness Tour. Ride in a Wheel Fun Rental at the Shoreline Village. Go on an app-led self-guided tour to the best Long Beach landmarks scavenger hunt. I’ve featured below the 6 top attractions, according to Tripadvisor, in Long Beach.

Aquarium of the Pacific

You’ll see frogs, the Ray Touchpool, Seals, Sea Lions, the Lorikeet Forest, Penguins, Sea Otters, Sharks, Jellies, Pacific Visions, and Coral Reefs. There are Exhibits and Habitats like the Harbor Terrace, the Ocean Science Center, the Southern California/Baja Gallery, the Northern Pacific Gallery, and the Tropical Pacific Gallery.

Catalina Express

You get to escape to Catalina Island that’s about an hour away from Long Beach. Catalina Express offers daily to and from ferry trips. On the island you’ll see Avalon town, Two Harbors village, and beaches. You can stay overnight if you decide to.

Naples Canals

Italian-inspired, rent kayaks, learn how to kayak, ride a gondola, hang out on a beach across from Naples, take a stroll down canal walkways, view beautiful houses, shop, and eat on 2nd Street.

Long Beach Waterfront

Restaurant Row on Pine Avenue in Downtown Long Beach, after dark is alive with colorful lights that illuminate historic buildings, music, and entertainment. Restored buildings shops galleries at the East Village Arts District, sea vessels at The Rainbow Harbor Esplanade, The Pike Outlets, and Shoreline Village.

The Queen Mary

Ocean liner, 1936 art deco, tours, restaurant, hotel, legendary, former WW2 troopship, retired in 1967, temporarily closed.

Shoreline Village

Long Beach Parasail, Marina Sailing, Pelican Pier Pavilion, Spirit Cruises, Wheel Fun Rentals, Smile for the Birdie, shopping, restaurants, Pirates Cove, Village Hats Shop, music, entertainment.
